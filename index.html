01
Research Snapshot

HUB Appliances is a B2B appliance supplier based in Medley, FL, founded in 2024 by Tomas Faverola, Samuel Malave, and Wilmer Morales. You serve developers, general contractors, and property managers across Miami-Dade, Broward, Palm Beach, and Naples. Your positioning: "Appliances Without the Headaches. On time. On budget. No surprises."

We analyzed your website, Instagram presence, TikTok, Google Business profile, review sites, and public business listings. We also researched your competitive landscape in the South Florida B2B appliance market.

You have grown to a multi-million-dollar operation in under two years with your own warehouse and delivery fleet. That growth happened without any formal marketing system. Here is what is holding back the next level.

5 Gaps We Found:

  1. Gap 1: Zero Social Proof Online. No Google reviews, no testimonials on your website, no case studies, no client logos. B2B buyers making decisions on 50-300 unit orders need proof that the supplier can deliver. Without it, every prospect evaluates HUB on a promise alone.
  2. Gap 2: No Content or Thought Leadership. No blog, no buying guides, no comparison content. Developers searching for "appliance packages for multi-family developments" or "bulk appliance supplier South Florida" cannot find you. Competitors who publish this content capture the lead before HUB enters the conversation.
  3. Gap 3: No Paid Acquisition or Retargeting. No evidence of paid ads on Meta, Google, or LinkedIn. No retargeting for website visitors. B2B appliance deals can be $500K+ for large developments. One converted developer can generate recurring revenue across multiple projects. The ROI on paid acquisition is significant.
  4. Gap 4: No Lead Nurture for Long Sales Cycles. Only a basic newsletter signup. No lead magnet. No email drip sequence. No CRM pipeline. Developers visit the site 6 months before their project breaks ground. Without nurture, HUB captures zero information from early-stage prospects.
  5. Gap 5: No Visual Credibility. No photos of completed projects, warehouse, delivery fleet, or team. You claim to handle 300-unit developments with an in-house fleet, but the website shows none of it. Developers want to SEE the operation before trusting it with their project.
